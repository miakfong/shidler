## Executive Summary 

This memo outlines a planned financial ratio analysis of Microsoft Corporation, a global leader in software, cloud computing, and digital services. The objective is to evaluate Microsoft's financial health using key accounting ratios to better understand its performance, efficiency, liquidity, and risk profile. While financial statements provide raw data, ratio analysis offers clearer insights into operational effectiveness, trends, and financial stability. This analysis will support informed decision making by identifying strengths, potential risks, and areas for improvement. The findings will ultimately guide strategic recommendations and provide a structured framework for evaluating Microsoft's recent financial performance.  

## Company Background & Objectives

Microsoft operates in the technology industry, with key business areas including cloud computing through Azure, productivity tools like Microsoft 365, and personal computing products. This analysis will focus on the company's most recent fiscal year.

A comprehensive ratio analysis is useful because it turns detailed financial data into clearer insights about how the company is performing. It helps leadership understand how profitable the business is, how efficiently it operates, how much risk it carries, and whether it can meet its short term obligations. With Microsoft continuing to grow in cloud services and expand its revenue streams, this analysis will highlight what's driving performance and whether that growth is sustainable. The goal is to provide insights that can support informed, strategic decision making at the executive level. 
## Approach

This analysis will use financial data from Microsoft's Form 10-K filings through the SEC EDGAR database, supplemented by sources such as Yahoo Finance and Microsoft's investor relations reports. The following ratio categories will be analyzed: performance, efficiency, profitability, leverage, liquidity, and DuPont analysis. Each category provides a different perspective on financial health, allowing for a well rounded evaluation. The results will be organized in an Excel model to keep the analysis consistent, accurate, and easy to interpret.
